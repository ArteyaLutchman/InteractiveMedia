Process Documentation

I was thinking about the interaction of dragging, I could only think about using the drag to look around on a canvas, or change the perspective of the user. I have forgotten about how seamlessly the drag element is implemented in web design. Some interactions I found are drawing, drag to scroll, sliders, drag to rotate, move elements, and other very experimental ideas.

At first, I wanted to mimic paper cut out on the webpage, where the user draws an image and then they are able to move the element around. But I found that to be very straightforward, and overall didnâ€™t like the idea too much. It was also a weird process to think about how the user would cut it out, and that cut out would become an object. I think that coding would be to indimdating for me.

Another idea I have is for the user to drag around the webpage, like scrolling to find weird items to put in their inventory. There would be a different theme of items, such as internet culture photos, horror items, and cute items. I didn't pursure this because I wanted to do something more abstract instead of game this time. Change the understanding of the drag element

Next would be combining sound, visuals and interactions. The user would drag the mouse around the screen, and depending on where the mouse is different visuals and sounds will play. A right click-drag would change the sound. I tried that with the final project, but for some reason I couldn't understand how to change the sound around without the user having to click. I didn't want the sound to be annoying either.

Finally, I settled on using momentum and somewhat physics while the user drags, as well as playing with colour. Mimicing momenum was pretty fun to do, I had to lookup how to do it exactly through p5.js, and look through examples to get excatly what I wanted. It was tricky to understand the code, but once I got through it, it was really nice.

I wanted to add a dragging to scroll element, but I only found examples that use installations/libaries of CSS's. If I was to futher to explore this interaction of dragging, I would add music or sound to make it more impactful. And not use Iframes, but instead have the sketch take up the whole screen. I did do hat before, but the canvas looked weird.
